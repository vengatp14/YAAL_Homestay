Herbs Discovered in Kodaikanal

Kodaikanal is a treasure trove of medicinal and aromatic herbs, thriving in its cool climate and pristine forests. Local communities and traditional healers have used these herbs for centuries, harnessing their natural healing powers for wellness and holistic health. Here are some of the most notable herbs found in Kodaikanal:

Shola Herbs

Unique to the shola forests, these herbs are used for treating fever, digestive issues, and skin ailments.

Eucalyptus

Widely used for its essential oil, eucalyptus helps with respiratory problems and acts as a natural antiseptic.

Wild Turmeric

Known for its anti-inflammatory and healing properties, wild turmeric is used in skin care and traditional medicine.

Nilgiri (Blue Gum)

Aromatic leaves used for their cooling effect and in herbal teas for soothing headaches and colds.

Lemon Grass

Popular for its refreshing aroma, lemon grass is used in teas and as a remedy for digestive and stress-related issues.

Vettiver (Khus)

Roots used for cooling drinks, perfumes, and as a natural remedy for skin and nerve disorders.

Indian Borage (Karpooravalli)

Leaves used for cough, cold, and throat infections, often brewed as a herbal tea.

Native Medicinal Roots

Traditional roots used in local remedies for boosting immunity and treating common ailments.

Other notable herbs found in Kodaikanal include:
  • Thulasi (Holy Basil): Used for respiratory and immune health.
  • Amla (Indian Gooseberry): Rich in vitamin C, boosts immunity.
  • Neem: Known for its antibacterial and detoxifying properties.
  • Arali (Oleander): Used in traditional medicine with caution.
  • Solanum trilobatum (Thuthuvalai): Used for asthma and respiratory issues.
  • Rauvolfia serpentina (Indian Snakeroot): Used for hypertension and calming nerves.
